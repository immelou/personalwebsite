My "Learning Korean Language" Journey

I spend most of my vacation in k-dramas and korean culture. Like the Netflix's Korea No.1 which I really love Lee Kwang Soo (from Running Man), Yu Jae Seok (Korea's most popular MC) and Kim Yeon Koung (Korea'snational volleyball player). The most thing I love in this show is they promote their country by learning on how koreans living their life from the start. Like building roofs from the temples and making a paint fresh from a tree sap which cost million dollar (possible that only chaebols has this expensive furniture). During my both watching and listening in koreans, I learned some words that are commonly heared when you are a k-drama fan like: "Annyeonghaseyo", "Hana Dul Set Ne Yeolso...", "Il, I, Ssam, Ssa...", "Oppa, Eomma, Eonni, Appa, Ahjussi, Ahjumma, Halmeonni" and many more. Hangul letters are so difficult to write, because of spelling, but you can easily learn how to read Hangul. Hoping for next year to learn more Hangul~! *finger-cross*
